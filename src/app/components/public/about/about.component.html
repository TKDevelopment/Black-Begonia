MEET BECCA

the creative behind Black Begonia Florals

Becca is the owner and lead designer of Black Begonia Floral Design, a boutique Rhode Island wedding florist creating refined, artful florals for celebrations across Newport, Providence, and coastal New England.

Her connection to flowers began early, growing up in Richmond, Rhode Island surrounded by gardens filled with peonies, lilacs, hydrangeas, and climbing clematis. Those quiet, formative moments—watching seasons change and beauty unfold naturally—continue to influence her work today.

At fifteen, she discovered floral design almost by accident, enrolling in a class that quickly revealed both a natural instinct and a deep creative pull. Under the guidance of a mentor who recognized her potential, she immersed herself in the craft—assisting with designs, refining her technique, and eventually creating her first wedding at just sixteen.

Her path led her to study Public Relations at the University of Rhode Island and later to New York, where she worked within established floral studios and event companies, gaining experience in luxury floral design and large-scale events. Along the way, she was mentored by talented women who helped shape both her artistic perspective and her vision for building something of her own.

In 2020, Becca returned to Rhode Island and founded Black Begonia Floral Design. What began as a creative leap soon evolved into a sought-after boutique studio. Today, she works closely with each couple from initial concept through installation, taking on a limited number of weddings each season to ensure every design feels deeply considered, cohesive, and personal.

Her work is defined by a balance of romance and intention—florals that feel natural yet elevated, expressive yet refined.

What I'm Passionate About...

Creativity...

Here at Black Begonia Floral Designs, creativity has always been at the center of my life, but it extends far beyond flowers. I love making and exploring in all forms—painting, writing poetry, listening to music, singing karaoke, or baking and decorating cookies and cupcakes. I thrive in spaces where creativity and fun come together, whether it’s a lively game of billiards or a spontaneous passion project.

In my mid-twenties I built a full luxury campervan myself, learning construction and electrical work along the way so I could travel and explore. Hands-on projects and figuring out how things work—something I definitely picked up from my dad—have always brought me joy. Creating something from start to finish is still one of my favorite experiences.

When I’m not designing flowers, I love spending time outdoors. Snowboarding and ice skating in the winter, roller skating in warmer months, hiking trails, playing disc golf, camping, or relaxing at the beach all help me recharge. Summer trips to Block Island with my brother and winter weekends visiting my parents in Vermont are always highlights.

All of these experiences—music, travel, baking, nature, creativity, and connection—keep me inspired and grounded. They influence the joy, artistry, and attention to detail I bring into every floral design. For me, passion is about embracing what makes life vibrant and bringing that energy into the work I love.

It simple but I love Block Island. Its a mini vacation, right in our backyard, where I can go travel to several times each summer. I love the beaches, the hiking trails, and the restuarants.
